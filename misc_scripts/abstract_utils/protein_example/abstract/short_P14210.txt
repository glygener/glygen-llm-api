The protein P14210-1 is the hepatocyte growth factor (HGF), with a mass of approximately 83,134 Da, expressed in humans. This protein plays a crucial role as a multifunctional cytokine, primarily involved in regulating cell growth, motility, and morphogenesis in various tissues. Notably, it activates the MET receptor, triggering signals essential for processes like angiogenesis and tissue regeneration. HGF is associated with diseases such as biliary tract benign neoplasm, limb ischemia, and various cancers, including colorectal, pancreatic, and skin cancers. The gene for HGF is known by several names, including HGF (recommended), DFNB39, and HGFB (synonymous). HGF undergoes significant post-translational modifications, including glycosylation and phosphorylation, enhancing its functionality. The count of glycosylation sites includes 10 identified in the extracellular region and 6 phosphorylations, reflecting its complex regulatory landscape. Moreover, HGF mutations have been linked to conditions like nonsyndromic deafness, and single nucleotide variants (SNVs) are recorded across various disease contexts. Overall, HGF emerges as a pivotal biomarker in oncological and regenerative medicine contexts, with substantial literature supporting its clinical relevance, including 58 publications, the latest discussing its association with COVID-19 in cancer patients.
